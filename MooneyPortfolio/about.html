A brief History

What is Grok?

Grok is a verb that comes from Robert A. Heinlein's 1961 book Stranger in a Strange Land and is a Martian word that translates most directly to English as "to drink", but connotes a complete, intimate, and intuitive understanding of an object or concept.

Why NeuroGrok?

I strive to grok the mysteries of perceptive experience by accumulating and manipulating knowledge of the nervous system.

Here are some of the ways that I strive to grok the nervous system:

  1. Teaching neuroscience to put known information about the nervous sytem into modern contexts
  2. Scientific research training to generate new knowledge
  3. Big data science to draw out hidden patterns in collection of data about brain function and dysfunction
  4. Data visualiztion that presents data in an intuitive format
